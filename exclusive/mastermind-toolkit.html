Mastermind Toolkit

Everything you need to run powerful mastermind groups: meeting agendas, hot seat frameworks, goal tracking, and accountability systems.

📚 Meeting Agenda Template (60 min)

  1. Check-in (5 min): Each member shares one win from the past week
  2. Accountability Review (10 min): Review commitments from last meeting. Did everyone follow through?
  3. Hot Seat #1 (15 min): One member presents their biggest challenge. Group brainstorms solutions.
  4. Hot Seat #2 (15 min): Second member gets the hot seat. Rotate each meeting.
  5. Resource Sharing (5 min): Share one tool, article, or insight that helped this week
  6. Commitments (10 min): Each member states 1-3 specific actions for the coming week

🔥 Hot Seat Framework

When you are in the hot seat, follow this structure:

  1. Context (2 min): Briefly describe your situation and what you have tried
  2. The Ask (1 min): State your specific question or challenge clearly
  3. Group Discussion (10 min): Others ask clarifying questions and offer perspectives
  4. Action Items (2 min): Summarize the top 2-3 actionable takeaways

Hot Seat Rules:

  • No judgment, only constructive input
  • Be specific with advice (what, when, how)
  • One person speaks at a time
  • Hot seat person takes notes, not the group

💡 Discussion Prompts

Use these prompts to spark deeper conversations:

  • What is the ONE thing that, if achieved this quarter, would make everything else easier?
  • What are you avoiding that you know you need to do?
  • If you had to 10x your results in 90 days, what would you change first?
  • What is the best investment you made in your business this month?
  • What would you do differently if you were starting over today?
  • Who do you need to become to reach your next revenue milestone?
  • What is the biggest time waste in your current workflow?
  • If your best customer had to describe you in 3 words, what would they say?
  • What is one thing you learned from a failure this month?
  • What is your unfair advantage that you are not leveraging enough?

🛠 How to Start a Mastermind Group

  1. Find 3-6 people at a similar stage and with complementary skills
  2. Set a schedule: Weekly or bi-weekly, same time, 60 minutes max
  3. Choose a platform: Zoom, Google Meet, or in-person
  4. Establish ground rules: Confidentiality, punctuality, participation
  5. Rotate roles: Facilitator, timekeeper, note-taker
  6. Use the hot seat format: Structured problem-solving beats open chat
  7. Track commitments: Write down and follow up on action items
  8. Review quarterly: Assess if the group is working. Adjust membership if needed.

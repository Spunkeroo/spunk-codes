Recording Setup Guide for Tutorials

A proper recording setup eliminates the most common quality problems before you press record. Follow these steps in order:

  1. Clean your desktop. Before every recording, close unnecessary windows, hide desktop icons, disable notification popups (Focus Assist on Windows, Do Not Disturb on macOS), and close any applications that might pop up during recording. A cluttered desktop makes tutorials look unprofessional and distracts viewers from the content.
  2. Set your display resolution. Record at 1920x1080. If you have a 4K monitor, scale your display to 1080p for recording. This ensures text and UI elements are large enough for viewers to read on mobile devices. Code tutorials should use a font size of at least 16px in the IDE.
  3. Configure your microphone. Use a USB microphone if you have one. Set the input level so your voice peaks at -12dB to -6dB in your recording software (this prevents clipping). In OBS, add a Noise Suppression filter (RNNoise) and a Compressor filter (ratio 3:1, threshold -18dB) to your microphone source.
  4. Choose your recording format. Record in MP4 with H.264 video codec and AAC audio codec. This produces universally compatible files that every editing application and upload platform accepts. Set video bitrate to 8,000-12,000 Kbps for 1080p and audio bitrate to 320 Kbps.
  5. Do a 30-second test recording. Record a quick test, play it back, and check: Is the audio clear? Is the screen readable? Is the frame rate smooth? Are there any notification popups? Fix issues before committing to a full tutorial recording.
  6. Prepare your script or outline. Even a simple bullet-point list of steps prevents you from rambling. Write down what you will show, what you will say, and the order of operations. Professional tutorials are planned, not improvised.

The Two-Monitor Advantage

If you have two monitors, record only one of them. Keep your script, notes, and recording controls on the second monitor. This lets you glance at your outline without it appearing in the recording. If you only have one monitor, use a tablet or phone on a stand next to your screen to display your script. This single change eliminates the long pauses, "um"s, and "what was I going to show next?" moments that make tutorials feel amateur.

Audio Quality Tips for Screen Recordings

Audio quality matters more than video quality in tutorials. Viewers will tolerate a slightly blurry screen recording with great audio. They will immediately close a crystal-clear recording with echoey, muffled, or noisy audio. Here is how to get professional audio for free:

Microphone Positioning

Position your microphone 6-12 inches from your mouth, slightly off to the side (not directly in front). This prevents plosive sounds (the burst of air on "P" and "B" sounds) from hitting the microphone capsule. If you are using a built-in laptop microphone, sit as close as possible and speak toward the keyboard area where the mic is typically located.

Room Treatment on a Budget

Record in a small room with soft surfaces. Closets full of clothes are acoustically excellent. Avoid large rooms with hard floors and bare walls, which create echo and reverb. Hang a thick blanket behind your monitor to absorb reflections from the wall you are facing. These simple changes make a $30 microphone sound like a $200 one.

Software Noise Reduction

Every free screen recorder supports post-recording noise reduction. In OBS Studio, the RNNoise filter removes background noise in real time during recording. In post-production, Audacity (free) has a Noise Reduction effect that samples a quiet section and removes that noise profile from the entire recording. Use noise reduction sparingly; too much creates a robotic, underwater sound.

5 Screen Recording Mistakes Beginners Make

  1. Recording without a plan. Hitting record and figuring it out as you go produces rambling, unfocused tutorials that viewers abandon within 30 seconds. Write at least a bullet-point outline of your steps before recording. Know what you will show, what you will say, and how you will transition between sections. A 5-minute planning session saves 30 minutes of re-recording.
  2. Ignoring audio quality. Bad audio is the number one reason viewers click away from tutorials. Echo, background noise, keyboard clicking, and quiet or muffled voice make tutorials unwatchable. Use a dedicated microphone (even a $26 USB mic), enable noise suppression, and record in a quiet room. Test your audio before every session.
  3. Recording at the wrong resolution. Recording at 720p produces tutorials that look pixelated on modern screens. Recording at 4K creates enormous files that are slow to edit and upload. Record at 1920x1080 (1080p) for the optimal balance. If your content includes small text (code, spreadsheets, settings menus), increase your font size or zoom in rather than recording at higher resolutions.
  4. Not showing your cursor. In tutorial recordings, the cursor is your pointer. Viewers follow it to understand where to click. Hide your cursor and viewers get lost. Enable cursor highlighting or a yellow circle around clicks in your recording software. In OBS, you can add a highlight effect around the cursor using browser source overlays. ShareX has built-in click visualization.
  5. Making tutorials too long. Viewers search for tutorials to solve a specific problem. They do not want a 45-minute video when the solution takes 3 minutes. Get to the point quickly, demonstrate the solution, and end. If your tutorial covers multiple topics, break it into separate videos or add chapters so viewers can skip to the relevant section. Aim for the shortest video that completely solves the problem.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best free screen recording tool for tutorials in 2026?
OBS Studio is the best overall free screen recording tool for tutorials in 2026. It is completely open source, supports unlimited recording length with no watermarks, records in up to 4K resolution, supports multiple audio and video sources, and includes scene transitions. For beginners who want something simpler, ShareX on Windows or the built-in macOS Screenshot toolbar are excellent alternatives that require zero configuration.
Can I record my screen for free without a watermark?
Yes. Several free screen recorders produce watermark-free recordings. OBS Studio, ShareX, and the built-in screen recorders on Windows 11 (Snipping Tool) and macOS (Screenshot toolbar) all record without watermarks. Many commercial tools like Loom and Screencastify add watermarks on their free tiers, but the open-source and built-in options do not. Always check before recording a full tutorial, as some tools only add watermarks when you export or share.
What resolution should I record tutorials in?
Record at 1920x1080 (1080p) for the best balance of quality and file size. This is the standard resolution for YouTube, Udemy, Skillshare, and most course platforms. If you are recording code or small text, 1080p ensures readability. Recording at 4K (3840x2160) is only necessary if your audience will view on large monitors or if you plan to crop and zoom in post-production. For social media tutorials (Instagram, TikTok), record at 1080x1920 (vertical 1080p).
Do I need a powerful computer to record screen tutorials?
For basic screen recording of presentations, web browsing, or document editing, most computers made after 2018 can handle recording smoothly. You need at least 8GB of RAM and a dual-core processor. If you are recording gameplay, live coding in a heavy IDE, or running screen recording alongside multiple applications, 16GB of RAM and a quad-core processor will prevent lag and dropped frames. OBS Studio uses hardware encoding (NVENC on NVIDIA, Quick Sync on Intel) which offloads the work from your CPU.
How do I record my screen and webcam at the same time for free?
OBS Studio is the best free option for simultaneous screen and webcam recording. Add your screen as a Display Capture source, then add your webcam as a Video Capture Device source. Position and resize the webcam overlay anywhere on the canvas. You can also use Loom's free tier (25 videos, 5 minutes each) for quick screen-plus-webcam recordings that are instantly shareable via link. On macOS, QuickTime Player can record screen with audio but does not support picture-in-picture webcam overlay natively.
What audio settings should I use for tutorial recordings?
Record audio at 48kHz sample rate and 320kbps bitrate for professional quality. Use a dedicated USB microphone if possible; the Audio-Technica ATR2100x and Samson Q2U both cost under $70 and sound dramatically better than built-in laptop microphones. In OBS Studio, enable noise suppression (RNNoise filter) to remove background noise in real time. Record in a quiet room, close windows, and turn off fans. If you must use a laptop microphone, sit within 12 inches of it and enable noise gate and noise suppression filters.

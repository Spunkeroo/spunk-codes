Local SEO Tools

If your business serves a local area, local SEO is where the money is. Local searches have a 28% conversion rate compared to 2-3% for non-local searches. That means local SEO traffic is 10x more valuable per visitor.

The core of local SEO is consistency: your business name, address, and phone number (NAP) must be identical everywhere. Your Google Business Profile must be complete and active. And your website must include location-specific pages and schema markup.

Here is how to use free tools for local SEO:

  1. Use the SEO Checklist to verify your site has LocalBusiness schema markup
  2. Generate location-specific content with the AI Writing Assistant
  3. Create service-area pages using the AI Blog Outline Generator
  4. Build local landing pages with optimized meta tags
  5. Use the Social Post Generator to create geo-targeted social content

Google Business Profile is free and is the single most important local SEO asset. Businesses with complete, active profiles appear in the Map Pack 70% more often than those with incomplete profiles. Claim yours at business.google.com if you have not already.

Best External Free SEO Tools

In addition to the tools on spunk.codes, these external platforms offer genuinely useful free SEO capabilities:

  • Google Search Console — The single most important free SEO tool. Shows which keywords you rank for, click-through rates, indexing status, mobile usability issues, and Core Web Vitals. Every website owner should have this set up on day one. Completely free, requires only site verification.
  • Google Analytics 4 — Tracks organic traffic, user behavior, conversion paths, and audience demographics. The data foundation for every SEO decision. Free for up to 10 million hits per month.
  • Google PageSpeed Insights — Analyzes your page load performance and gives specific recommendations for improvement. Based on Lighthouse data. Core Web Vitals scores directly impact rankings.
  • Bing Webmaster Tools — Similar to Google Search Console but for Bing. Often overlooked, but Bing handles 10-15% of search traffic and has less competition. Also provides backlink data that Google does not share.
  • Ahrefs Free Webmaster Tools — Free site audit and backlink checker for verified sites. Limited compared to the paid version but still useful for identifying technical issues and monitoring your backlink profile.
  • AnswerThePublic (free tier) — Visualizes questions people ask about any topic. Three free searches per day. Excellent for finding long-tail keyword opportunities and FAQ content ideas.
  • Google Trends — Compare keyword popularity over time, identify seasonal trends, and find rising topics. Free and unlimited. Essential for content planning and trend-based SEO.
  • Screaming Frog SEO Spider (free version) — Crawls up to 500 URLs and identifies broken links, duplicate content, missing meta tags, and redirect chains. The industry standard for technical SEO audits. Free version handles most small-to-medium sites.

The Free SEO Workflow (Step by Step)

Here is the exact SEO workflow you can follow using only free tools:

Phase 1: Foundation (Week 1)

  1. Set up Google Search Console — verify your site and submit your sitemap
  2. Install Google Analytics 4 — set up conversion tracking for your key goals
  3. Run a full site audit — use the SEO Checklist on every important page
  4. Generate your XML sitemap — use the Sitemap Generator and submit to Search Console
  5. Fix all critical issues — broken links, missing meta tags, duplicate content

Phase 2: Content (Weeks 2-4)

  1. Research keywords — use AnswerThePublic and Google autocomplete to find opportunities
  2. Plan content with the Content Planner — map topics to keywords and search intent
  3. Generate outlines with the AI Blog Outline Generator — ensure SEO structure
  4. Write content with the AI Writing Assistant — then add your expertise
  5. Optimize each page — run the SEO Checklist before publishing

Phase 3: Growth (Ongoing)

  1. Publish 2-4 optimized posts per week — consistency beats volume
  2. Repurpose content — use the Content Repurposer for multi-channel distribution
  3. Build internal links — every new post links to 3-5 existing pages
  4. Monitor rankings — check Search Console weekly for trends
  5. Update existing content — refresh top pages quarterly with new data and examples

SEO Launch Checklist for New Sites

Launching a new website? Here is every SEO task you need to complete before going live, in order of priority:

  1. Install SSL certificate — HTTPS is a confirmed ranking factor. Most hosts provide this free.
  2. Set up Google Search Console — submit your domain for verification immediately.
  3. Create and submit XML sitemap — use the Sitemap Generator.
  4. Write unique title tags for every page — 50-60 characters, primary keyword near the front.
  5. Write unique meta descriptions for every page — 150-160 characters, include a call to action.
  6. Ensure one H1 tag per page — and it should match the page's primary keyword target.
  7. Add alt text to every image — descriptive, keyword-relevant, and useful for screen readers.
  8. Create a robots.txt file — allow all pages you want indexed, block admin and staging areas.
  9. Implement canonical tags — prevent duplicate content issues from URL parameters and trailing slashes.
  10. Set up 301 redirects for any old URLs — preserve link equity from previous domains or URL structures.
  11. Test mobile responsiveness — Google uses mobile-first indexing. Your mobile version is your primary version.
  12. Check page speed — aim for sub-3-second load times. Use the CSS Minifier to reduce file sizes.
  13. Add schema markup — at minimum, Organization and WebSite schemas on your homepage.
  14. Verify internal linking structure — every page should be reachable within 3 clicks from the homepage.
  15. Install analytics — Google Analytics 4 and Microsoft Clarity for quantitative and qualitative data.

SEO Myths to Ignore in 2026

SEO is plagued by outdated advice and myths that refuse to die. Here are the ones that will waste your time if you follow them:

Myth: "You need to hit an exact keyword density"

There is no magic keyword density percentage. Google uses semantic understanding, not keyword counting. Write naturally, cover the topic thoroughly, and include related terms. If you are writing comprehensively about a topic, the keywords will appear at an appropriate frequency naturally.

Myth: "AI content gets penalized"

Google has explicitly stated that AI-generated content is not inherently penalized. What gets penalized is thin, unhelpful content regardless of how it was produced. AI content that includes genuine expertise, unique examples, and real value ranks just as well as human-written content. The key is adding what AI cannot: your experience, your data, your perspective.

Myth: "Backlinks are everything"

Backlinks still matter, but their relative importance has decreased significantly as Google's understanding of content quality has improved. In 2026, a page with zero backlinks but exceptional content that perfectly matches search intent can and does outrank pages with hundreds of backlinks but mediocre content. Focus on content quality first, link building second.

Myth: "You need paid tools to do SEO"

The tools listed in this guide cover every essential SEO task. Paid tools provide convenience and scale, but they do not unlock capabilities that are unavailable for free. Google Search Console alone provides more actionable SEO data than most paid tools did five years ago. Start with free tools, and only upgrade to paid when your traffic volume justifies the investment.

Myth: "SEO is dead because of AI search"

AI search features like Google's AI Overviews and Bing's Copilot are changing how search results appear, but they are not eliminating organic traffic. AI Overviews often increase curiosity and drive clicks to the sources they cite. Optimize your content to be cited by AI systems by providing clear, factual, well-structured information with unique data and perspectives.

"The best SEO strategy in 2026 is the same as it has always been: create the most helpful content on the internet for your specific topic. The tools just make it faster to do well."

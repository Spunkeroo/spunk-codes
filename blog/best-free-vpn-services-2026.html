Best Free VPN Services in 2026

A VPN is no longer optional in 2026. Whether you are protecting your browsing on public Wi-Fi, bypassing geographic restrictions, or keeping your ISP from selling your data, a virtual private network encrypts your traffic and masks your IP address. The good news is that several reputable VPN providers now offer genuinely useful free tiers with no hidden catches, no shady data harvesting, and no bandwidth throttling during normal use.

This guide compares the four best free VPN services available right now: ProtonVPN, Windscribe, hide.me, and Cloudflare WARP. We tested each one for speed, privacy policies, server availability, streaming compatibility, and real-world usability. Every detail listed here has been verified as of February 2026.

ProtonVPN Free

Best for: Privacy-focused users who need unlimited data

Free tier: Unlimited data with no bandwidth caps. Access to servers in 5 countries: United States, Netherlands, Japan, Romania, and Poland. Single device connection on the free plan. Supports Windows, macOS, Linux, Android, iOS, and ChromeOS.

How to access: Download from protonvpn.com and create a free Proton account. No credit card required. The same account works across Proton Mail, Proton Drive, and Proton Calendar.

Strengths: ProtonVPN is the gold standard for free VPN privacy. It is operated by Proton AG, the Swiss company behind Proton Mail, and is subject to Switzerland's strict privacy laws. The free tier has genuinely unlimited data, which is unique among trustworthy free VPNs. The no-logs policy has been independently audited by Securitum. ProtonVPN uses the WireGuard protocol by default, which provides excellent security with minimal speed overhead. The kill switch ensures your real IP never leaks if the VPN connection drops. ProtonVPN is also one of the few VPN providers that is fully open-source, with all client applications published on GitHub.

Limitations: Free users are limited to a single device connection. The 5-country server selection is limited compared to paid plans. Speeds can slow during peak hours because free servers share capacity with a large user base. Free users cannot access Secure Core (multi-hop) servers, NetShield ad-blocking, or the Tor over VPN feature. Streaming services like Netflix and Disney+ actively block ProtonVPN free servers.

Typical speeds: 40-80 Mbps download on nearby servers. 15-40 Mbps on distant servers. Sufficient for HD streaming, video calls, and general browsing. Not ideal for 4K streaming or large file downloads during peak hours.

Windscribe Free

Best for: Users who want streaming access and flexibility

Free tier: 10 GB per month with email registration (2 GB without email). Earn an extra 5 GB by posting about Windscribe on X (Twitter). Access to servers in 11 countries including US, Canada, UK, France, Germany, Netherlands, Switzerland, Romania, Denmark, Hong Kong, and Norway. Unlimited simultaneous device connections.

How to access: Download from windscribe.com. Create a free account with just a username and password (email optional but adds 8 GB). Available on Windows, macOS, Linux, Android, iOS, and as browser extensions for Chrome, Firefox, and Edge.

Strengths: Windscribe is the most feature-rich free VPN available. The 11-country server selection is the largest among free tiers. Unlike most free VPNs, Windscribe allows unlimited simultaneous connections, so you can protect your phone, laptop, and tablet simultaneously. The built-in ad blocker (R.O.B.E.R.T.) blocks ads, trackers, and malware at the DNS level on all connections. The browser extension includes additional features like cookie auto-deletion, GPS spoofing on mobile, and split tunneling. Windscribe's free servers can unblock some streaming content that other free VPNs cannot access. The no-logs policy has been verified through an independent audit.

Limitations: 10 GB per month is enough for everyday browsing and email but not enough for heavy streaming or large downloads. The data resets on the first of each month. Some streaming platforms like Netflix have blocked most Windscribe free servers, though the Windflix servers on the paid plan still work. P2P traffic is allowed but counts toward your data cap. The free plan does not include the static IP or port forwarding features.

Typical speeds: 60-120 Mbps download on nearby servers. 30-70 Mbps on distant servers. Generally faster than ProtonVPN free because of lower server congestion and WireGuard protocol support.

hide.me Free

Best for: Torrenting and P2P file sharing

Free tier: 10 GB per month. Access to servers in 8 countries: United States (East and West), Canada, Netherlands, Germany, France, Singapore, and United Kingdom. Single device connection. No registration required to start using the service.

How to access: Download from hide.me. You can start using the VPN immediately without creating an account. Creating a free account unlocks the full 10 GB allowance and server selection. Available on Windows, macOS, Linux, Android, iOS, Fire TV, and routers.

Strengths: hide.me is one of the very few free VPNs that explicitly allows P2P and torrenting on its free servers. Most free VPNs block torrent traffic entirely, making hide.me the clear choice for users who need to download files via BitTorrent safely. The no-registration option is excellent for users who want quick, anonymous protection without providing any personal information. hide.me is based in Malaysia, which has no mandatory data retention laws. The no-logs policy has been independently audited. The client supports WireGuard, OpenVPN, IKEv2, and SoftEther protocols, giving users flexibility to choose based on their needs. Split tunneling allows you to route only specific apps through the VPN.

Limitations: 10 GB per month can be consumed quickly when torrenting large files. Single device connection limits flexibility. The server network is smaller than Windscribe's free offering. Streaming unblocking is inconsistent on free servers. The free plan does not include the SOCKS5 proxy, dedicated IP, or multi-hop features available on paid plans.

Typical speeds: 50-100 Mbps download on nearby servers. 25-60 Mbps on distant servers. P2P speeds typically range from 30-70 Mbps, which is fast enough for most torrent downloads.

Cloudflare WARP

Best for: Maximum speed and mobile users

Free tier: Unlimited data with no bandwidth caps. Routes traffic through Cloudflare's global network spanning 300+ cities worldwide. Available on Windows, macOS, Linux, Android, and iOS through the 1.1.1.1 app. No registration required.

How to access: Download the 1.1.1.1 app from your app store or warp.plus. Toggle WARP on. No account creation, no email, no credit card. It works immediately.

Strengths: WARP is the fastest free option by a wide margin. Because Cloudflare operates one of the largest networks in the world, your traffic is routed through the nearest Cloudflare data center, minimizing latency. In many cases, WARP actually improves your connection speed compared to your raw ISP connection because Cloudflare's routing is more efficient than typical ISP routing. The app is extremely simple with a single on/off toggle. WARP encrypts all traffic between your device and the Cloudflare network using WireGuard-based protocol (BoringTun). No data caps, no bandwidth limits, and no speed throttling make it the most generous free offering in terms of raw capacity. It is excellent for securing mobile connections on public Wi-Fi.

Limitations: WARP is not a traditional VPN in the privacy sense. It does not let you choose your server location, so you cannot bypass geographic restrictions on streaming services. Cloudflare can see your traffic metadata (which sites you visit), though they state they do not sell this data or use it for advertising. WARP does not hide your approximate location from websites because it routes traffic through the nearest data center rather than a distant server. There is no kill switch on the free tier. It does not support P2P traffic. WARP is best understood as an encryption and performance tool rather than a privacy or anonymity tool.

Typical speeds: 100-300 Mbps download depending on your ISP speed. Often within 5-10% of your raw internet speed. WARP adds minimal overhead because traffic exits at the nearest Cloudflare edge, unlike traditional VPNs that may route through distant servers.

Privacy and Logging Policies

Privacy is the primary reason most people use a VPN. Here is exactly what each provider logs and what they do not.

ProtonVPN - Strictest privacy

ProtonVPN maintains a strict no-logs policy verified by independent audits from Securitum. They do not log connection timestamps, IP addresses, session duration, DNS queries, or traffic data. Based in Switzerland, which has no mandatory data retention laws and sits outside the Five Eyes, Nine Eyes, and Fourteen Eyes intelligence-sharing alliances. Proton has a documented history of refusing law enforcement requests for user data because they genuinely do not have it. All apps are open-source.

Windscribe - Strong privacy with transparency

Windscribe logs the total bandwidth used in a 30-day period (necessary to enforce the free tier cap) but does not log connection timestamps, IP addresses, visited websites, or any traffic content. Based in Canada, which is a Five Eyes member, though Windscribe has publicly fought government data requests and publishes a transparency report. The no-logs policy has been independently audited. All apps are open-source.

hide.me - Independently verified zero logs

hide.me maintains a certified no-logs policy audited by DefenseCode. They do not store connection logs, IP addresses, timestamps, or bandwidth usage (the 10 GB cap is tracked locally on the device). Based in Malaysia, which has no data retention laws. hide.me publishes an annual transparency report showing they have never provided user data to any government because they do not collect it.

Cloudflare WARP - Privacy-light

Cloudflare WARP logs some connection metadata including the amount of data transferred and error diagnostics. They commit to not logging your browsing activity, DNS queries, or the content of your traffic. However, Cloudflare is a US-based company subject to US law, including potential National Security Letters. WARP's privacy policy is less strict than dedicated VPN providers. It is best for encryption and performance rather than anonymity.

Free VPNs to Avoid

For every trustworthy free VPN, there are dozens of dangerous ones. These free VPN services have documented histories of privacy violations and should be avoided entirely.

Red flags in free VPNs

  • Hola VPN: Uses your device as an exit node for other users' traffic. Your IP address can be used for activities you did not authorize. This has been documented since 2015 and the practice continues.
  • SuperVPN: Multiple security researchers have found the app contains malware, transmits unencrypted data, and leaks user information. It has been removed from app stores multiple times.
  • Betternet: Documented by CSIRO researchers to contain tracking libraries and malware. The app injects ads and tracks your browsing.
  • Any VPN requesting excessive permissions: A VPN should not need access to your contacts, phone calls, camera, or file storage. If it does, it is harvesting data to sell.
  • VPNs with no identifiable company: If you cannot find a real company name, office address, and executive team behind the VPN, do not trust it with your traffic.

The rule is simple: If a free VPN has no clear business model (like Proton's freemium approach or Cloudflare's network optimization strategy), then you are the product. Your browsing data is being collected and sold to advertisers, data brokers, or worse.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are free VPNs safe to use in 2026?

The four VPNs reviewed in this guide are safe. ProtonVPN, Windscribe, and hide.me have all undergone independent security audits confirming their no-logs policies. Cloudflare WARP is operated by one of the largest internet infrastructure companies in the world. However, the vast majority of free VPNs on app stores are not safe. They fund operations by collecting and selling your data, injecting ads, or using your device as part of a botnet. Stick to the providers in this guide or other well-known, audited services.

Can I use a free VPN for Netflix?

In practice, free VPNs are unreliable for Netflix in 2026. Netflix aggressively blocks VPN IP addresses, and free servers are the easiest targets because they serve many users from the same IP addresses. Windscribe occasionally works with some Netflix libraries, but access is inconsistent and can break at any time. If unblocking streaming is your primary goal, a paid VPN service is the realistic solution.

Is Cloudflare WARP a real VPN?

WARP encrypts your traffic and routes it through Cloudflare's network, which provides security benefits similar to a VPN. However, it does not change your apparent geographic location, does not have a kill switch on the free tier, and Cloudflare can see metadata about your connections. WARP is best described as a secure DNS and traffic encryption service. It protects you from ISP snooping and public Wi-Fi attacks but does not provide the anonymity or geo-shifting that traditional VPNs offer.

Which free VPN has the best speed?

Cloudflare WARP is the fastest by a significant margin, retaining over 85% of your raw connection speed. This is because WARP routes traffic through the nearest Cloudflare data center rather than a distant VPN server. Among traditional VPNs, Windscribe is the fastest free option, followed by hide.me. ProtonVPN free is the slowest due to higher server congestion but still provides speeds adequate for HD streaming and video calls.

Can my employer or school see what I do with a VPN?

A VPN encrypts your traffic so your employer or school network administrator cannot see the content of your browsing. However, they can see that you are using a VPN, which may violate acceptable use policies. Some networks block VPN connections entirely. If you are on a company-managed device, your employer may have monitoring software installed that can see your activity regardless of VPN usage. A VPN protects your traffic on the network level but cannot override device-level monitoring installed by an administrator.

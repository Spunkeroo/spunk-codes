The 6 Pipeline Stages

1. Lead

New leads that haven't been qualified yet. First contact, inbound inquiries, cold outreach responses.

2. Qualified

Leads you've spoken with who have budget, authority, need, and timeline (BANT). Ready for a proposal.

3. Proposal

You've sent a proposal or quote. Waiting for review and questions.

4. Negotiation

Active discussions about price, terms, or scope. Both sides working toward agreement.

5. Closed Won

Deal signed. Revenue recognized. Time to celebrate.

6. Closed Lost

Deal didn't close. Track why to improve future conversion.

Set Probabilities Realistically

Most sales teams overestimate win probability. Use these benchmarks: Lead (10%), Qualified (25%), Proposal (50%), Negotiation (75%), Closed Won (100%), Closed Lost (0%). The weighted pipeline value uses these probabilities to forecast revenue.

Pipeline Stats Dashboard

The built-in stats dashboard calculates automatically:

  • Total Deals — active deals across all stages
  • Pipeline Value — sum of all deal values (excluding closed lost)
  • Weighted Value — pipeline value adjusted by probability percentages
  • Win Rate — percentage of deals that close won vs total closed deals
  • Average Deal Size — mean value across all active deals
  • Won Revenue — total value of Closed Won deals

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I customize the pipeline stages?

The current version has 6 fixed stages that cover the standard B2B sales process. Custom stages may be added in a future update.

Can I assign deals to team members?

The tool is designed for individual use. Each team member can run their own pipeline in their browser. For team visibility, use the CSV export to consolidate data.

How is weighted pipeline value calculated?

Each deal's value is multiplied by its probability percentage. A $10,000 deal at 50% probability contributes $5,000 to the weighted pipeline value. This gives you a more realistic revenue forecast.

Does drag and drop work on mobile?

HTML5 drag and drop has limited mobile support. On mobile devices, you can change a deal's stage by editing the deal card. The Kanban view works best on desktop or tablet.

Tips for Getting Better AI Writing Output

  1. Be specific about your audience — "Write a blog post about SEO" produces generic content. "Write a 2,000-word guide to technical SEO for ecommerce store owners who know basic SEO but need help with site architecture" produces content that actually resonates with a specific reader.
  2. Provide examples of the style you want — Paste a paragraph of writing you admire and ask the AI to match that style. This produces far better results than describing the style in abstract terms.
  3. Edit AI output aggressively — AI-generated first drafts are starting points, not finished products. The best AI-assisted content is 60 percent AI draft and 40 percent human editing. Your expertise, voice, and original insights are what make the content valuable.
  4. Use multiple AI tools for different stages — Perplexity for research, Claude for drafting, ChatGPT for brainstorming variations. Each tool has different strengths. Using the right tool for each stage produces better output than using one tool for everything.
  5. Add your own data and experience — AI cannot provide your unique experiences, case studies, results, or opinions. The most valuable content combines AI efficiency with human expertise. Always add your own insights, data, and real-world examples to AI-generated frameworks.
  6. Check facts independently — AI writing tools hallucinate. They present fabricated statistics, fake quotes, and invented references with complete confidence. Always verify specific claims, numbers, dates, and quotes against reliable sources before publishing.
  7. Iterate on prompts, not just output — If the output is not what you want, change your prompt rather than editing the result. A better prompt produces a better starting point, which requires less editing. The AI Prompt Vault helps you find prompts that are already optimized.

The 80/20 Rule for AI Writing

AI writing tools handle 80 percent of the grunt work — research, structure, first drafts, SEO optimization, and repurposing. The remaining 20 percent — original insights, personal voice, fact-checking, and strategic editing — is where humans add irreplaceable value. Do not try to automate everything. Focus your time on the 20 percent that makes content unique.

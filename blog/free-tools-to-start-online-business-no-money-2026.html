Free Hosting and Domain Setup

Hosting is the first expense most people assume they need. Traditional web hosting costs $5 to $30 per month, and many beginners end up paying for overpriced shared hosting plans that deliver terrible performance. In 2026, you do not need to pay for hosting at all.

Free — Unlimited

GitHub Pages

What it is: Free static site hosting from GitHub. Supports custom domains, automatic SSL certificates, and deploys directly from a Git repository. Each repository gets a free .github.io subdomain, or you can connect your own domain.

Best for: Portfolios, blogs, landing pages, documentation sites, single-page applications, and tool collections. The entire SpunkArt network of 120+ sites is hosted on GitHub Pages at zero cost.

Limits: 1 GB repository size, 100 GB bandwidth per month, 10 builds per hour. For context, 100 GB of bandwidth serves approximately 1 million page views per month for a typical website.

Free — Unlimited

Cloudflare Pages

What it is: Free static and serverless hosting with a global CDN. Supports unlimited bandwidth on the free tier, automatic SSL, custom domains, and serverless functions for dynamic features.

Best for: Businesses that need serverless backend functions (form handling, API calls, database queries) alongside their frontend. Cloudflare Pages is more flexible than GitHub Pages because it supports server-side logic.

Limits: 500 builds per month, 100,000 serverless function invocations per day. These limits are extremely generous for any new business.

Free — No Limits

Cloudflare DNS and SSL

What it is: Free DNS management, DDoS protection, and SSL certificate provisioning. Cloudflare sits between your domain registrar and your hosting, providing a security and performance layer at no cost.

Best for: Every website. There is no reason not to use Cloudflare's free DNS. It is faster than most registrar DNS, includes basic DDoS protection, and provisions SSL certificates automatically.

For domain registration itself, you will need to spend approximately $10 to $15 per year on a .com domain through registrars like Namecheap, Porkbun, or Cloudflare Registrar (which charges at-cost prices with no markup). This is the only expense in this entire guide that cannot be avoided if you want a professional domain name. However, you can start with a free .github.io subdomain and upgrade to a custom domain later when revenue allows.

Pro Tip: The Free Domain Strategy

Start with yourname.github.io. Build your site, create content, and start getting traffic. Once you have your first sale or your first 1,000 visitors, invest the $12 in a .com domain and point it to the same GitHub Pages site. Zero downtime, zero configuration headaches, and you only spend money after you have proven the concept works.

Free Email Marketing Tools

Email marketing has the highest ROI of any marketing channel. The Direct Marketing Association consistently reports returns of $36 to $42 for every $1 spent on email. But when you are starting at $0, even a $13/month Mailchimp plan feels like a lot. Fortunately, several platforms offer genuinely useful free tiers.

Free — 500 Contacts

Mailchimp Free Plan

What it is: Email marketing platform with landing pages, signup forms, basic automation, and audience management. The free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 email sends per month.

Best for: Absolute beginners who want a drag-and-drop email builder with templates. Mailchimp's interface is the most intuitive in the industry, and their deliverability rates are among the highest.

Limitations: Only one audience (list), limited automation (single-step only), Mailchimp branding on emails, no A/B testing, no send-time optimization.

Free — Unlimited Contacts

Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

What it is: Email marketing, SMS marketing, and CRM platform. The free plan allows unlimited contacts with 300 emails per day (approximately 9,000 per month).

Best for: Businesses that expect to grow their list beyond 500 contacts quickly. Brevo's unlimited contacts on the free tier is a significant advantage over Mailchimp. It also includes basic CRM functionality and transactional email capabilities.

Limitations: Daily sending limit of 300, Brevo branding on emails, limited automation workflows, no landing page builder on free tier.

Free — 2,500 Subscribers

MailerLite Free Plan

What it is: Email marketing with a drag-and-drop editor, landing pages, pop-ups, and basic automation. Free plan supports up to 1,000 subscribers with 12,000 emails per month.

Best for: Creators and bloggers who want landing pages and signup forms included with their email marketing. MailerLite's free tier is the most feature-complete of any email platform.

The strategy here is straightforward: start with Mailchimp if you are brand new to email marketing and want the easiest setup. Switch to Brevo when you approach the 500-contact limit. The migration takes about 30 minutes by exporting your contacts as a CSV and importing them into the new platform.

Free Design and Branding Tools

Professional design used to require expensive Adobe subscriptions and years of training. In 2026, AI-powered design tools and browser-based editors have made professional-quality design accessible to everyone. Here are the tools that replace hundreds of dollars in design software.

Free — Generous Limits

Canva Free

What it is: Browser-based design platform with thousands of templates for social media posts, presentations, logos, business cards, flyers, infographics, videos, and more. Includes a drag-and-drop editor, stock photos, icons, and fonts.

Best for: Non-designers who need professional-looking graphics quickly. Canva's template library means you never start from a blank canvas. Select a template, customize the colors and text, and export. The entire process takes 5 to 15 minutes for most designs.

Free tier includes: 250,000+ templates, 1 million+ stock photos and graphics, 5 GB cloud storage, basic photo editing, and real-time collaboration.

Free — Full Featured

Figma Free

What it is: Professional UI/UX design tool used by teams at Google, Microsoft, and most major tech companies. The free plan includes 3 Figma files and unlimited personal files, real-time collaboration, prototyping, and access to the community plugin library.

Best for: Designing websites, apps, and user interfaces. If you are building a web application or need to create detailed mockups before development, Figma is the industry standard and its free tier is remarkably generous.

Free — No Limits

Photopea

What it is: A free browser-based image editor that supports PSD, XCF, Sketch, XD, and CDR file formats. It is essentially a free Photoshop clone that runs entirely in your browser with no installation required.

Best for: Advanced image editing tasks that Canva cannot handle. Photo retouching, layer-based compositing, batch processing, and working with PSD files from designers or clients.

Free Analytics and Tracking

You cannot improve what you do not measure. Analytics tells you where your visitors come from, what they do on your site, and where they leave. Without analytics, every business decision is a guess. These tools give you enterprise-grade insights at zero cost.

Free — No Limits

Google Analytics 4 (GA4)

What it is: The most widely used web analytics platform in the world. Tracks visitors, sessions, page views, conversions, traffic sources, user demographics, and hundreds of other metrics. Integrates with Google Ads, Search Console, and BigQuery.

Why you need it: GA4 answers the fundamental questions: How many people visit your site? Where do they come from? What pages do they visit? Do they convert? Without these answers, you are flying blind.

Setup time: 10 minutes. Add a single JavaScript snippet to your site header. If you are using GitHub Pages, paste it into every HTML file or use a static site generator that injects it automatically.

Free — Unlimited

Microsoft Clarity

What it is: Free behavioral analytics tool that records user sessions and generates heatmaps. Watch exactly how visitors interact with your pages: where they click, how far they scroll, where they hesitate, and where they leave.

Why you need it: GA4 tells you what happened. Clarity tells you why. When you see a high bounce rate on a page, Clarity shows you the session recording that reveals the user could not find the call-to-action button, or the page took too long to load, or the content did not match their expectations.

Setup time: 5 minutes. Add a JavaScript snippet alongside your GA4 code. Clarity also integrates directly with GA4 for combined insights.

Free — No Limits

Google Search Console

What it is: Shows you exactly which Google searches lead people to your website, your average position for each keyword, click-through rates, indexing status, and technical SEO issues.

Why you need it: This is the single most important tool for organic search growth. It provides first-party data directly from Google about how your site performs in search results. No paid tool can replicate this data because only Google has it.

Free Invoicing and Payments

Getting paid should not cost money. Several platforms offer free invoicing, payment processing, and basic accounting without monthly fees. You only pay transaction fees when you actually receive money, which is the fairest possible model for a new business.

Free — Full Featured

Wave

What it is: Free accounting and invoicing software designed for small businesses. Includes unlimited invoicing, receipt scanning, financial reporting (income statements, balance sheets, cash flow), bank connections, and basic payroll. The core platform is completely free.

Best for: Freelancers and service businesses that need professional invoices and basic bookkeeping. Wave replaced QuickBooks for thousands of small businesses when Intuit raised prices to $30+/month.

Revenue model: Wave makes money from optional payment processing (2.9% + $0.60 per credit card transaction) and payroll services. The accounting and invoicing software itself is genuinely free with no limits.

Free — 5 Clients

Zoho Invoice Free

What it is: Professional invoicing with automated payment reminders, time tracking, expense tracking, and multi-currency support. Free for up to 5 customers and 1,000 invoices per year.

Best for: Businesses with a small number of high-value clients. If you are a consultant, freelance developer, or agency with fewer than 5 active clients, Zoho Invoice Free provides everything you need.

Free Setup — Per-Transaction Fees

Stripe

What it is: Payment processing platform used by millions of businesses worldwide. No monthly fees, no setup costs. You pay 2.9% + $0.30 per successful card charge. Supports subscriptions, one-time payments, invoicing, and 135+ currencies.

Best for: Any online business that sells products or services. Stripe's developer tools are the best in the industry, but their no-code payment links and hosted checkout pages make it accessible to non-technical founders too.

Free Productivity and Project Management

Running a business means juggling dozens of tasks, deadlines, and priorities. These free tools keep you organized without the $10-25/month project management subscriptions.

Free — Generous Limits

Notion Free

What it is: All-in-one workspace that combines notes, documents, databases, project boards, wikis, and calendars. The free plan includes unlimited pages and blocks for individual use.

Best for: Solo founders who need a single place to manage everything: business plans, content calendars, task lists, meeting notes, SOPs, and knowledge bases. Notion replaces 5-6 separate tools.

Free — 10 Boards

Trello Free

What it is: Visual project management using Kanban boards. Drag cards between columns to track tasks through stages like "To Do," "In Progress," and "Done." Free plan includes unlimited cards, up to 10 boards, and basic automation.

Best for: Visual thinkers who prefer seeing their workflow laid out spatially. Trello's simplicity is its strength — there is virtually no learning curve.

Free — Full Featured

Google Workspace (Free Tier)

What it is: Google Docs, Sheets, Slides, Forms, Drive (15 GB), Gmail, Calendar, and Meet. All free with a Google account. Real-time collaboration, version history, and offline access.

Best for: Document creation, spreadsheets, presentations, and basic collaboration. Google Docs replaces Microsoft Word, Sheets replaces Excel, and Forms replaces paid survey tools like Typeform.

The 30-Day Launch Plan

Knowing which tools to use is one thing. Executing is another. Here is a realistic 30-day plan to go from zero to launched using only free tools:

Week 1: Foundation

  • Day 1-2: Define your business idea and target audience. Write a one-page business plan in Google Docs.
  • Day 3-4: Register a GitHub account, create a repository, and deploy your first page to GitHub Pages.
  • Day 5-6: Set up Cloudflare DNS for your domain (or use the free .github.io URL). Install GA4 and Clarity.
  • Day 7: Design your logo and brand colors using Canva and the SpunkArt Color Palette Generator.

Week 2: Build

  • Day 8-10: Build your website homepage, about page, and one product or service page.
  • Day 11-12: Create your email signup form with Mailchimp or Brevo. Add it to every page.
  • Day 13-14: Set up your invoicing with Wave. Create your first invoice template. Set up Stripe for online payments.

Week 3: Content

  • Day 15-17: Write 3 blog posts targeting long-tail keywords you found with Ubersuggest and Google Trends.
  • Day 18-19: Create social media accounts. Design 10 posts in Canva. Schedule them with Buffer.
  • Day 20-21: Run an SEO audit on every page. Fix all issues identified. Submit your sitemap to Google Search Console.

Week 4: Launch

  • Day 22-24: Create a launch announcement email. Send it to your initial list (friends, family, professional contacts).
  • Day 25-26: Share your launch on social media, relevant forums, and communities. Ask for feedback.
  • Day 27-28: Review GA4 and Clarity data. Identify what is working and what needs improvement.
  • Day 29-30: Write 2 more blog posts based on keywords that are already showing impressions in Search Console.

By day 30, you have a live website, email marketing set up, social media presence established, analytics tracking everything, payment processing ready, and content driving organic traffic. Total cost: $0 (or $12 if you bought a domain).

Mistakes to Avoid When Starting With Free Tools

Free tools are powerful, but there are common pitfalls that trip up new entrepreneurs:

1. Tool Hopping

Do not spend weeks evaluating 15 different email platforms. Pick one from this list, start using it, and move on. You can always switch later. The cost of indecision is far greater than the cost of choosing the "wrong" free tool.

2. Over-Designing Before Launching

Your website does not need to be perfect to launch. A clean, functional page with clear information about what you offer and how to buy it is enough. You can redesign later when you have actual customer feedback to guide your decisions.

3. Ignoring Analytics From Day One

Install GA4 and Clarity before you launch, not after. The data you collect in your first month is invaluable for understanding how early visitors interact with your site. If you wait three months to add analytics, you have lost three months of insights.

4. Not Building an Email List Immediately

Your email list is the only audience you truly own. Social media algorithms change. Search rankings fluctuate. But your email list is yours forever. Start collecting emails on day one, even if you do not have anything to send yet. When you do have something to sell, your list is ready.

5. Treating "Free" as "Temporary"

These tools are not training wheels. GitHub Pages hosts production websites serving millions of visitors. GA4 powers analytics for Fortune 500 companies. Stripe processes billions of dollars in payments. Free does not mean inferior. Use these tools with confidence.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you really start an online business with no money?

Yes. In 2026, free tools cover every essential category: hosting (GitHub Pages, Cloudflare Pages), email marketing (Mailchimp, Brevo), design (Canva, Figma), analytics (GA4, Clarity), invoicing (Wave), and payments (Stripe, PayPal). The only potential cost is a domain name at $10-15/year, and even that can be avoided by starting with a free .github.io subdomain. Thousands of profitable businesses run entirely on free tool stacks.

What is the best free hosting for a new online business?

GitHub Pages is the best starting point for most businesses. It is completely free, supports custom domains, includes automatic SSL, and deploys from a Git repository. For businesses that need server-side functionality (dynamic forms, API endpoints), Cloudflare Pages adds serverless functions at no cost. Between the two, you can host virtually any type of website without paying for traditional hosting.

Is free email marketing effective for small businesses?

Absolutely. Mailchimp's free plan supports 500 contacts and 1,000 sends per month. Brevo offers 300 emails per day with unlimited contacts. MailerLite gives you 1,000 subscribers with 12,000 monthly sends. For a new business building its initial audience, these limits are more than sufficient. The quality of your emails matters far more than the platform you send them from.

What free design tools can replace Adobe Creative Suite?

Canva replaces Photoshop and InDesign for most business design needs (social media, presentations, marketing materials). Figma replaces Sketch and Adobe XD for UI/UX design. Photopea is a browser-based Photoshop clone that handles advanced image editing. GIMP provides desktop-class image editing. DaVinci Resolve offers professional video editing for free. Together, these tools cover 95% of what small businesses need from the Adobe suite.

How do I accept payments without paying for software?

Stripe and PayPal both offer free account setup with no monthly fees. You only pay per-transaction fees when you actually receive money (typically 2.9% + $0.30 per charge). Wave provides free invoicing with optional payment processing. Square offers a free point-of-sale app for in-person sales. The key insight is that modern payment processors only charge when you get paid, so there is zero upfront cost.

What free analytics tools should every new business use?

Three tools form the essential analytics stack: Google Analytics 4 (GA4) for traffic and conversion tracking, Google Search Console for SEO performance and keyword data, and Microsoft Clarity for session recordings and heatmaps. Install all three on day one. GA4 shows you what is happening, Search Console shows how Google sees your site, and Clarity shows you why visitors behave the way they do. Together, they provide more insight than most paid analytics suites.

When should I upgrade from free tools to paid ones?

Only upgrade when a specific free tool limitation is directly costing you money or preventing growth. For example, upgrade email marketing when you exceed the free contact limit and your list is generating revenue. Upgrade hosting only if you exceed bandwidth limits (unlikely for most businesses). Never upgrade preemptively or "just in case." Many successful businesses with six-figure revenue still run primarily on free tool stacks.

Is GitHub Pages reliable enough for a real business?

Yes. GitHub Pages is backed by Microsoft's infrastructure and has a 99.9%+ uptime track record. It serves static files from a global CDN, which means fast load times worldwide. The SpunkArt network runs 120+ production websites on GitHub Pages serving millions of page views. Major open-source projects, documentation sites, and businesses rely on it daily. The "free" label does not indicate inferior reliability.

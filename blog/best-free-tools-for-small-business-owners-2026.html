Best Free Invoicing Tools for Small Business

Invoicing is the lifeblood of any small business. If you cannot invoice efficiently, you cannot get paid efficiently. The good news is that free invoicing tools in 2026 handle everything from recurring invoices to automatic payment reminders — features that used to require expensive subscriptions.

1. Wave — Best Overall Free Invoicing

What it does: Wave provides unlimited free invoicing with professional templates, automatic payment reminders, receipt scanning via mobile app, and a direct connection to its free accounting module. You can create and send invoices in under 60 seconds, accept online payments (2.9% + $0.60 per transaction for credit cards), and track which invoices are overdue.

Why it is the best: Truly unlimited — no cap on invoices, clients, or users. The integration with Wave's free accounting means your invoices automatically sync to your books. Payment processing is optional (the invoicing itself is free). The mobile app lets you invoice from anywhere.

Free tier includes: Unlimited invoices, unlimited clients, receipt scanning, recurring invoices, automatic reminders, financial reporting.

Best for: Freelancers, service businesses, consultants, and any small business that invoices clients regularly.

Try Wave Free

2. Zoho Invoice — Best for International Businesses

What it does: Zoho Invoice is a free invoicing platform that supports multiple currencies, multiple languages, and tax systems from over 50 countries. It handles time tracking, expense tracking, project-based billing, and customer portals where clients can view and pay invoices.

Why it stands out: The multi-currency and multi-language support makes it ideal for businesses with international clients. The customer portal is a premium feature you rarely see in free tools — clients get their own login to view invoices, make payments, and communicate. Time tracking is built in, so service businesses can track hours and convert them to invoices instantly.

Free tier includes: 5 clients, multi-currency invoicing, time tracking, expense tracking, customer portal, payment reminders.

Best for: International businesses, agencies with global clients, service businesses that bill by the hour.

Try Zoho Invoice

3. Invoice Ninja — Best Open Source Option

What it does: Invoice Ninja is an open-source invoicing platform you can self-host for complete control or use their free cloud version. It supports invoicing, quoting, expense tracking, time tracking, project management, and payment processing through Stripe, PayPal, and 40+ other gateways.

Why it matters: Being open source means you can customize it to your exact needs. The self-hosted version has zero limitations. Even the free cloud tier is generous with up to 20 clients. The proposal and quoting features turn it from a simple invoicing tool into a full client management system.

Free tier includes: 20 clients (cloud), unlimited (self-hosted), invoicing, quoting, proposals, expense tracking, time tracking, 40+ payment gateways.

Best for: Tech-savvy business owners who want full control, businesses that need extensive customization.

Try Invoice Ninja

Best Free Accounting Software for Small Business

Accounting is where most small business owners either overpay or under-manage. You do not need QuickBooks at $30/month when free accounting software in 2026 can handle everything from double-entry bookkeeping to tax-ready financial reports. Here are the free tools that actually deliver.

4. Wave Accounting — Best Free Cloud Accounting

What it does: Wave offers a complete double-entry accounting system for free. Bank connections, automatic transaction categorization, financial statements (income statement, balance sheet, cash flow), receipt scanning, sales tax tracking, and journal entries. It connects directly to Wave Invoicing for a seamless financial picture.

Why it leads: Wave is the only truly free accounting software that includes bank connections and automatic categorization. Most competitors limit these features to paid tiers. The reports are clean, export-ready, and your accountant will have no trouble working with them during tax season.

Free tier includes: Unlimited income and expense tracking, bank connections, receipt scanning, financial reports, journal entries, sales tax tracking.

Try Wave Accounting

5. GnuCash — Best Desktop Accounting (Open Source)

What it does: GnuCash is a free, open-source desktop accounting application that supports double-entry bookkeeping, accounts receivable and payable, stock and mutual fund portfolios, and financial reports. It has been developed for over 20 years and is used by millions of small businesses worldwide.

Why it is relevant in 2026: While cloud tools dominate, GnuCash remains the gold standard for privacy-conscious business owners who want their financial data on their own computer. It supports import from QIF, OFX, and CSV files, handles multiple currencies, and generates every standard financial report you would need.

Price: Completely free, forever. No tier, no upsell, no data collection.

Download GnuCash

6. Akaunting — Best Self-Hosted Cloud Accounting

What it does: Akaunting is a free, open-source online accounting software for small businesses. It provides invoicing, expense tracking, bank connections, financial reports, inventory tracking, and a module system that lets you add features as you need them. You can self-host it or use their cloud service.

Why it is worth considering: Akaunting fills the gap between Wave (cloud but closed source) and GnuCash (open source but desktop). Self-host it on a $5/month server and you have a full accounting system with no per-user fees, no feature limits, and complete data ownership.

Price: Free (self-hosted). Cloud from $0/month with optional paid modules.

Try Akaunting

Best Free Project Management Tools

Project management tools keep your tasks, deadlines, and team organized. In 2026, the free tiers of major project management platforms are so generous that most small businesses never need to upgrade. Here are the tools that give you the most capability at zero cost.

7. Trello — Best for Visual Task Management

What it does: Trello uses a Kanban board system with cards, lists, and boards to organize anything from product launches to content calendars. Power-Ups (integrations) connect it to Slack, Google Drive, Jira, and hundreds of other tools. Butler automation lets you create rules, buttons, and scheduled commands to automate repetitive tasks.

Why it remains essential: Trello is the most intuitive project management tool ever built. Drag a card, move it to "Done" — that is it. The free tier now includes unlimited cards, up to 10 boards per workspace, unlimited Power-Ups per board (this used to be paid), and Butler automations. For solo entrepreneurs and small teams, this is more than enough.

Free tier includes: Unlimited cards, 10 boards/workspace, unlimited Power-Ups, 250 Butler automations/month, unlimited storage (10MB/file).

Try Trello Free

8. Notion — Best All-in-One Workspace

You can build custom CRMs, project trackers, content calendars, SOPs, and knowledge bases using their block-based editor. AI features help you write, summarize, and organize content.

Why it dominates: No other tool provides this level of flexibility for free. The free plan includes unlimited pages, unlimited blocks for individuals, and sharing with up to 10 guests. Templates let you clone entire business systems in seconds. The AI assistant (limited free usage) can draft emails, summarize meeting notes, and translate documents.

Free tier includes: Unlimited pages and blocks (individual), 10 guest collaborators, 5MB file uploads, 7-day page history, AI assistant (limited).

Try Notion Free

9. ClickUp — Best Feature-Rich Free PM Tool

What it does: ClickUp is the Swiss Army knife of project management. It supports tasks, docs, goals, time tracking, whiteboards, mind maps, sprints, and Gantt charts — all in one platform. Views include list, board, calendar, timeline, table, and workload. The free tier is absurdly generous compared to competitors.

Why small businesses love it: You get features that competitors charge $25+/user/month for. The free plan includes unlimited tasks, unlimited members, 100MB storage, and most core features. The learning curve is steeper than Trello, but the payoff is a single tool that replaces 3-4 others.

Free tier includes: Unlimited tasks, unlimited members, 100MB storage, docs, whiteboards, sprints, goals, time tracking (basic), Gantt charts, calendar view.

Try ClickUp Free

10. Asana — Best for Growing Teams

What it does: Asana is a structured project management tool designed for teams. It supports tasks, subtasks, sections, milestones, and multiple views (list, board, calendar, timeline). Integrations with 200+ tools and a mobile app make it accessible anywhere.

Why it belongs here: Asana's free tier supports up to 10 team members with unlimited tasks, projects, messages, and file storage (100MB/file). The structure encourages accountability — every task has an assignee and a due date. It is the best free option for teams that need clear ownership and deadlines.

Free tier includes: 10 team members, unlimited tasks and projects, list/board/calendar views, integrations, mobile app, 100MB file storage per file.

When to Upgrade from Free

Free tools are perfect for starting and growing, but watch for these signals that it is time to upgrade:

  • Team growth: When you exceed free user limits (e.g., 10 users on Asana, 3 users on Freshsales)
  • Storage limits: When 15GB of Google Drive or 5GB of Canva storage is not enough
  • Automation needs: When you need advanced workflows, custom integrations, or API access
  • Contact limits: When your email list exceeds Mailchimp's 500-contact free tier
  • Compliance needs: When you require SSO, audit logs, or enterprise-grade security

Until you hit these walls, stay free. Every dollar saved on software is a dollar you can invest in marketing, product, or people.
